CMECH is a startup developing a modular system to capture CO2 from seawater

Powered by ocean waves our machine produces solid carbon.

About Us

Know what we do

  • What we do

    CMECH is researching the development of a machine that combines CO2 removal from seawater, CO2 reduction using electrolysis, and wave power in a single modular design. Scalability is achieved by producing more machines. Carbon is removed from the carbon cycle and used to promote plant growth on land.

  • Our Goal

    Is to process thousands of tons of CO2 per year per machine. By improving and increasing the number of machines, and utilizing the vast energy resource of Ocean waves, we can seriously scale carbon removal for a real impact on the Global greenhouse gas emissions.

How we do it

With the power of nature

CO2 from Seawater

CMECH's carbon capture technology removes CO2 from seawater using a double tank system. The first tank lowers the pH to bubble out the CO2. The second tank increases the pH to release the water back to sea.

Concentrated CO2 to solid Carbon

CO2 gas produced is electrolyzed to remove the oxygen and separate out a solid carbon precipitate. The carbon is collected and brought to land.

Land Reclamation

The carbon can be prepared to have high porosity and made into a soil additive that holds water and nutrients in the soil. Highly stable, this carbon persists in the ground for centuries while promoting environmental sustainability and ecosystem revival.

We need a solution

The planet is getting hotter.

The surface temperatures of the Earth are increasing. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) has made it clear that in order to meet the Paris Agreement target of maximum 2 degrees C, then Negative emissions will be necessary.
Know the reasons

Why we need it

Higher surface temperatures

Increase wildfires and expand deserts. Over the past 100 years the Sahara has grown 920,000 km2 - an area larger than France and Germany combined.

Carbon for Land Reclamation

* Improves the fertility of soils
* Provides habitats for beneficial soil bacteria and fungi.
* Improves the moisture retention.
* Improves nutrient retention.
* Increases soil volume.
* Removes carbon from the carbon cycle.

Increased vegetation

Cools ground temperatures through shade, water release, CO2 absorption, slowing hot winds, and increase precipitation. Increased tree cover supports biodiversity and helps restore ecosystems.
